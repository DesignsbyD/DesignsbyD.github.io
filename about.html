About Me

Darleny is a Dominican American designer and storyteller from the Bronx, currently completing her degree in Business Communication with a concentration in Graphic Communications at Baruch College. Her work lives at the heart of design, community, and creativity, shaped by meaningful roles in nonprofit cultural organizations such as El Museo del Barrio, the Center for Puerto Rican Studies, and now Commonpoint, where she is a Creative Design Fellow. These experiences taught her that great design is more than visuals. It is a joyful mix of empathy, clarity, and intention that helps people feel seen and supported.

On campus, Darleny served as the Director of Community Outreach for ALPFA at Baruch, where she helped bring career opportunities and community building to students across the campus. She also created LatAm Travels, a mentorship program that guides students through fully funded study abroad opportunities across Latin America. This work grew out of her own life changing semester at Universidad Iberoamericana in the Dominican Republic, where she rediscovered her roots and her artistic voice.

Outside of the studio, you can usually find Darleny practicing languages, reading, or chatting with friends from around the world. She is conversational in Portuguese, continues to deepen her Spanish through creative work, and is slowly exploring Korean and Mandarin as part of her dream to one day work across both APAC and LATAM regions.

Energetic, imaginative, and rooted in community, Darleny hopes to pursue a career in Graphic Design, Product Design, or UX Design. She is excited to create work that inspires, informs, and brings people together in meaningful ways.
